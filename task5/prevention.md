# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students in India

Cybercrime is growing fast. College students like you are often targeted. You are online all the time. Studying, scrolling, paying, chatting. This makes you an easy target. This checklist will help you stay online.

## General Prevention Tips

**1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords**

Think of your password like a lock on your hostel room. You wouldn't use the lock for everything. Mix small letters, numbers and symbols. Don't use your name, birthdate or easy numbers like "1234". A password manager can help you remember all your passwords.

**2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**

If someone gets your password 2FA will stop them. Turn it on for your email, Instagram, banking apps and other important accounts. It only takes a minute to set up. Can save you a lot of trouble.

**3. Think Twice Before Clicking**

Get a message saying you won a prize? Your package is stuck? Your account will be blocked? Don't rush. Scammers try to make things feel urgent. Don't click links. Download files from people you don't know. Whether its WhatsApp, Telegram, SMS or email.

**4. Verify Before You Share Anything Personal**

Your Aadhaar, PAN passport details and personal photos are private. If a website or person asks for these. It feels off trust your instincts. Legitimate services rarely ask for documents out of the blue.

**5. Keep Your Devices and Apps Updated**

Those update notificationsre easy to ignore.. They often have important security fixes. An outdated app is like a window. Someone will find it. Set updates to happen if you can.

**6. Check Your App Permissions**

Open your phone settings. See what your apps can access. Does a flashlight app really need your contacts? Does a game need your location? Remove permissions that don't make sense. Less access means risk.

## UPI & Financial Safety Tips

**7. Never Share Your UPI PIN, OTP or Card Details. Ever**

No bank, government office or customer care agent will ever ask for your UPI PIN or OTP. If someone does hang up. Stop responding. It's a scam.

**8. Use UPI Apps Carefully**

Some habits to save your money:

- Always read the payee name carefully before confirming a payment

- Turn on SMS and email alerts for every transaction

- Set a limit on transfers, from your bank account

- Never scan a QR code sent by a stranger. Scammers use QR codes to pull money from your account.
